1. Ganges River Ghats:

The ghats along the Ganges River are the heart of Varanasi. Witness the spiritual rituals, ceremonies, and bathing rituals that take place at sunrise and sunset.

2. Kashi Vishwanath Temple:

One of the most revered Hindu temples in India, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It's known for its intricate architecture and spiritual significance.

3. Sarnath:

A significant Buddhist pilgrimage site where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon. Explore ancient stupas, monasteries, and the Ashoka Pillar

4. Boat Ride on Ganges:

Experience the tranquility of the Ganges by taking a boat ride during sunrise or sunset. It offers a unique view of the ghats and the rituals taking place.

5. Evening Ganga Aarti:

Attend the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti ceremony at Dashashwamedh Ghat, where priests perform rituals with fire lamps, incense, and chants.

6. Banarasi Sari Shopping:

Varanasi is renowned for its intricate Banarasi silk sarees. Explore local markets to find a stunning range of traditional and contemporary designs.

7. Ramnagar Fort:

A historic fort located on the opposite bank of the Ganges. It houses a museum with antique artifacts, vintage cars, and a unique collection of weapons.

8. Bharat Kala Bhavan:

This museum within Banaras Hindu University showcases an impressive collection of sculptures, paintings, textiles, and archaeological artifacts.

9. Kachori and Jalebi at Kashi Chaat Bhandar:

: Indulge in Varanasi's famous street food by trying Kachori and Jalebi at Kashi Chaat Bhandar, a local culinary hotspot.

10. Bathing Ghats:

: Beyond the popular ghats, explore the various bathing ghats each with its own unique significance and rituals.
