Amer Fort
This majestic fort is an epitome of Rajput architecture and offers panoramic views of the city. The intricate carvings, stunning courtyards, and the Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors) are truly captivating
Hawa Mahal
Known as the "Palace of Winds," this iconic structure is famous for its unique facade with numerous windows that allowed royal women to observe street life without being seen.
City Palace
This grand palace complex showcases a blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture. It houses museums, courtyards, and the Chandra Mahal, still inhabited by the royal family.
Jantar Mantar
A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this astronomical observatory boasts a collection of 19 architectural instruments designed for astronomical observations.
Jaipur Literature Festival
The world's largest free literary festival, JLF brings together renowned authors, poets, and intellectuals for discussions, debates, and cultural exchange.
Albert Hall Museum
This museum showcases an impressive collection of artifacts, including miniature paintings, sculptures, costumes, and even an Egyptian mummy.
Johari Bazaar
Known for its vibrant gemstone and jewelry shops, this market offers a stunning array of precious and semi-precious stones, traditional jewelry, and handicrafts.
Rajasthani Cuisine
Indulge in the rich flavors of Rajasthani cuisine, including dishes like dal baati churma, ghevar, and the famous Rajasthani thali.
Jal Mahal
This "Water Palace" is located in the middle of Man Sagar Lake and offers a stunning view against the backdrop of the Aravalli hills.
Local Markets
Bapu Bazaar, Johari Bazaar, and Tripolia Bazaar are vibrant marketplaces where you can shop for textiles, jewelry, handicrafts, and more.
